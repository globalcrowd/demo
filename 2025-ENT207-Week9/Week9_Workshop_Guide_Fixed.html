üß™ Testing Protocol (8-10 minutes per app)

Install/access the app. What's your immediate reaction? Is the purpose clear from the moment you open it? Would you understand what to do without any explanation?

Try to complete the main user task without help. Note any confusion, friction points, bugs, or unclear labels. Where did you get stuck? What felt smooth?

Rate overall experience (1-5 stars). Document specific issues with screenshots/notes. Provide constructive suggestions with concrete alternatives.

Pretend you just discovered this app in the wild. No context beyond what's in the interface! The team should NOT explain anything - watch them struggle to learn where the UX needs work!

‚Ä¢ Take notes on paper/phone during testing
‚Ä¢ Give your written notes directly to the app team (NOT to instructors)
‚Ä¢ Focus on specific, actionable feedback
‚Ä¢ ‚úÖ Good: "The button text 'Submit' is unclear - try 'Create Account' instead"
‚Ä¢ ‚ùå Bad: "The app is confusing"

üîß Phase 2: Quick Fixes (20 minutes)

After receiving feedback, prioritize your fixes:

Priority 1: Fix anything that completely breaks the app or prevents core functionality
Priority 2: Clear up the most confusing user experience issues that multiple testers mentioned
Priority 3: Quick wins - better button labels, clearer instructions, improved copy

üí° Focus on 1-2 fixes that will make the biggest difference! You don't have time for everything.
